MA
What's behind the loan-to-share ratio being elevated — is it member lending growth or share runoff?
CR
Answer · 5300 Schedules A + B · linked quarters

Primarily member lending growth, not share runoff. Total shares grew ~4.1% YoY (healthy), but the loan portfolio grew ~9.8% — driven by a 23% surge in auto loans and 11% growth in real estate loans. Share growth has been adequate but lending is outpacing it. This is a common pattern in rate-rise cycles where members seek term loans to lock in before further hikes. Watch the liquidity coverage ratio and contingency funding plan — the trend is manageable now but worth flagging in your ALM narrative.
